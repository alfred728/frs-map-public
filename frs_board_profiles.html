Background

David Geraghty is a B.Eng-qualified metallurgical engineer with over 30 years in Western Australian resources. Before joining FRS he spent 21 years at Mineral Resources Limited (ASX: MIN), serving as Executive General Manager of Process Minerals International and then Director of Technology and Innovation.[8] He was there for the full arc of MinRes growing from a private contract crushing business into one of Australia's largest diversified miners. Earlier in his career he worked in processing and orebody evaluation roles at BHP, Hamersley Iron, and Rio Tinto in the Pilbara.[1]

Key Achievements at MinRes
  • Named inventor on patent WO2011044608A1: a commercial dense medium cyclone and WHIMS process for recovering manganese from ore fines previously discarded as tailings, deployed at scale in MinRes operations.[9]
  • Drove the design and manufacture of MinRes's 1,000 tonne-per-hour mobile crushing and screening plants, which became a primary revenue driver for the mining services business.[11]
  • Led the public rollout of MinRes's world-first fully autonomous road train program for the Onslow Iron project in the Pilbara.[10]

Why This Matters for FRS

FRS's near-term production path hinges on converting the Lake Johnston nickel processing facility to gold. That is precisely what Geraghty spent his career on: mineral processing plant design, beneficiation circuit development, and large-scale operations. His 21-year MinRes network also opens doors to contractors, offtake, and capital that would take a junior explorer decades to build independently. He has backed his conviction with over A$2.7M in personal on-market purchases.

Background

Brett Hodgins is a Western Australian geologist with over 25 years across Pilbara iron ore operations, WA gold exploration, and resource sector advisory. He started his career commissioning world-scale iron ore mines for Robe River and Rio Tinto[16] before spending 11 years as President and CEO of Central Iron Ore Ltd (TSX-V listed), running exploration on a high-grade gold project in the Yandal Greenstone Belt.[13] His combined BSc Geology (Hons) and GradDip Applied Finance from FINSIA is unusual for a field geologist and allows him to evaluate assets in both technical and investment terms.[4]

Hodgins explored the South Darlot project in the Yandal-Goldfields province, the same geological terrane as FRS's Bonnie Vale, Coolgardie, and Mt Palmer projects. His concurrent NED role at Beacon Minerals also gave him direct prior familiarity with the MacPhersons Reward asset before FRS acquired it.

Background

Daniel Raihani is an accountant, registered tax agent, and entrepreneur who built a 20-year private business career spanning accounting, real estate, manufacturing, and international finance across Sydney, UAE, and Hong Kong before entering the ASX listed-company space in 2023. Since then he has taken on board positions at seven ASX companies, consistently arriving with personal capital and deal flow. His most significant transaction to date was structuring and executing the approximately A$60 million Aurumin/Brightstar Resources merger[20] as Managing Director of Aurumin, which consolidated the Sandstone Greenstone Belt under single ownership for the first time in over a decade.

The Aurumin/Brightstar Merger
  • Joined Aurumin as a substantial shareholder via 7 Enterprises Pty Ltd before joining the board, the same approach used at FRS.[17]
  • Elevated to MD and CEO after founder Brad Valiukas stepped down in November 2024.[18] Immediately divested A$6M of non-core assets to sharpen the portfolio.[19]

  • Scheme effective 21 November 2025. Aurumin delisted 3 December 2025.[21]

Why This Matters for FRS

Raihani has already run the exact playbook FRS is executing: a regional goldfield consolidation transaction structured as an all-scrip scheme, creating a resource of scale from two previously separate juniors. The Aurumin/Brightstar deal is a direct precedent for FRS's Lake Johnston hub strategy.[20] His accounting and tax credentials add financial governance depth to the board, and his pattern of personal capital commitment across every board role aligns his interests squarely with shareholders.

"This merger reduces risks associated with holding a single project and delivers substantial synergy benefits that wouldn't be possible as separate entities."
